Washington State Data Breach Analysis & Visualization

By Chase Chemero  ·  cchemero@usfca.edu
Background & Motivation

In an increasingly digital world, data privacy has become a paramount concern for citizens and legislators alike. Washington State requires organizations to report data breaches that affect its residents, creating a rich dataset for public analysis. This project stems from a desire to understand the evolving landscape of cybersecurity. By visualizing this data, the goal is to uncover whether data breaches are becoming more frequent, which industries are most vulnerable, and how long it typically takes for an organization to notify the public after a breach occurs.

Project Objectives

The primary goal of this visualization is to allow users to explore the severity and nature of data breaches in Washington.

Objective 1

Temporal Trends

Users will be able to identify if the frequency and scale (number of residents affected) of breaches are increasing over time.

Objective 2

Breach Categorization

Users will be able to determine which types of breaches (e.g., Hacking, Phishing, Unauthorized Access) are most prevalent.

Objective 3

Risk Assessment

Users will be able to identify which types of personal information (SSN, Health info, Financial data) are most frequently compromised.

Objective 4

Accountability Analysis

Users will be able to visualize the "Notification Gap" — the time elapsed between the date of the breach and the date residents were notified.

Visualizations
Design 1

Temporal Dashboard

Interactive area chart with brush-and-zoom showing residents affected by month over the full dataset range.
Design 2

Categorical Network

Node-link diagram connecting organization types to the categories of information stolen in each breach.
Design 3

Heatmap View

Calendar heatmap showing breach density and notification lag intensity by year and month.
Final Design

Integrated Dashboard

Coordinated multi-view dashboard with a timeline, bar chart, donut chart, and treemap with cross-filtering across all views.
